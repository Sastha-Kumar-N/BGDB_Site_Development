Nelumbonaceae

The Nelumbonaceae, commonly known as the lotus family, is a small but intriguing family of flowering plants. It includes two genera: Nelumbo and the recently described extinct genus Archaeanthus. The most famous member of this family is the sacred lotus, Nelumbo nucifera. Lotuses are aquatic plants with distinctive circular leaves and stunning, fragrant flowers. They are culturally significant in many parts of Asia, representing purity, enlightenment, and rebirth in various religious and philosophical traditions. The lotus is also valued for its edible seeds and rhizomes, which are used in Asian cuisine and herbal medicine. Nelumbonaceae members are adapted to aquatic habitats, growing in still or slow-moving waters like ponds, lakes, and marshes. Their unique combination of beauty, cultural importance, and ecological adaptability makes them a captivating and valued plant family in both horticultural and symbolic contexts. 

Nelumbo nucifera.

The lotus, scientifically known as Nelumbo nucifera, is a remarkable and culturally significant aquatic plant that holds a special place in various societies around the world. This iconic plant is renowned for its elegant beauty and deep symbolism. Lotuses are characterized by their large, round leaves that float on the water's surface and their exquisite, fragrant flowers that rise above the surface on tall stalks.
