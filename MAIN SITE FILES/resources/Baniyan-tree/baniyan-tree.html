Ficus religiosa

 Ficus religiosa, commonly known as the sacred fig or bodhi tree, belongs to the Moraceae family within the Plantae kingdom. This culturally revered species, classified as Ficus religiosa, holds religious significance in Hinduism and Buddhism, being associated with the enlightenment of Siddhartha Gautama, the Buddha. With its distinctive heart-shaped leaves and intricate aerial roots, the sacred fig stands as an iconic symbol. Beyond its religious importance, the tree plays a crucial ecological role, providing habitat and sustenance for various wildlife through its fruits. Its taxonomic ranking reflects its place in the Plantae kingdom, Magnoliophyta class, and Moraceae family, emphasizing its dual significance in both the natural world and spiritual traditions.
