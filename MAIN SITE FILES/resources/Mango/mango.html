Mangifera indica

Mangifera indica, is taxonomically classified as follows: Kingdom Plantae, Phylum Angiosperms, Class Eudicots, Order Sapindales, Family Anacardiaceae, Genus Mangifera, and Species indica. As a member of the Anacardiaceae family, Mango shares evolutionary ties with other fruit-bearing plants like cashew and pistachio. The classification highlights its position among flowering plants that produce seeds enclosed within fruits, with distinctive features placing it within the Sapindales order. The genus Mangifera encompasses various tropical fruit trees, and the specific epithet "indica" reflects Mango's South Asian origin. Understanding this taxonomic hierarchy aids not only in botanical identification but also in broader ecological and agricultural contexts, providing a foundation for scientific research and conservation efforts..
