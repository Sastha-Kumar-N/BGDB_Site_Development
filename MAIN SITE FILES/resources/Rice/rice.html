Oryza sativa

Ocimum sanctum, commonly known as Holy Basil or Tulsi, holds significant medicinal and molecular importance. Revered in traditional medicine systems like Ayurveda, Tulsi has been recognized for its diverse therapeutic properties. Its leaves contain essential oils rich in compounds such as eugenol, rosmarinic acid, and ocimene, contributing to its antimicrobial, anti-inflammatory, and antioxidant effects. Studies have highlighted Tulsi's potential in managing various health conditions, including respiratory disorders, stress, and metabolic imbalances. Moreover, the molecular constituents of Tulsi have been subject to scientific investigation, revealing potential applications in drug development and molecular medicine. The plant's adaptogenic and immunomodulatory properties further underscore its relevance in modern healthcare, emphasizing the enduring significance of Ocimum sanctum in both traditional and molecular medicinal contexts.
