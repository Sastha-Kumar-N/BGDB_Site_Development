Rutaceae

The scientific name for the orange plant is Citrus x sinensis. It belongs to the Rutaceae family. The orange tree is an evergreen tree that can grow to be 16–50 ft (5–15 m) tall. It has a rounded crown and elliptical or oval leaves. Orange trees are grown for their fruits, flowers, and as ornamentals. There are two main types of oranges: sweet and bitter. The scientific name for the sweet orange is Citrus sinensis. The scientific name for the bitter orange is Citrus aurantium.

Citrus sinensis.

Citrus sinensis is a small, evergreen tree that is also known as the orange or sweet orange. It is a hybrid of the pomelo (Citrus maxima) and mandarin (Citrus reticulata).

Citrus aurantium

Citrus aurantium is an evergreen tree that can grow up to 5 meters tall. It's also known as: Sour orange, Bitter orange, Seville orange, Bigarade. Citrus aurantium is native to Southeast Asia (Chinese and Myanmar). It's cultivated in Southern Europe and in regions with a subtropical climate
