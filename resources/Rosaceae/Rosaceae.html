Rosaceae

The Rosaceae family, also known as the rose family, is one of the most important plant families in the world. It includes over 3,000 members, including: Roses, Geum, Alchemilla, Potentilla, Blackberries, Raspberries, Cloudberries, Dewberries, Loganberries. Plants in the Rosaceae family can be: Herbs, vines, shrubs, or trees Annual, perennial, or everlasting Deciduous or evergreen Armed with thorns or prickles Erect, branched, hard, and woody

Other characteristics of Rosaceae plants include: Leaves that are arranged alternately along the stem A pair of small, leaf-like appendages (called stipules) on the stem at the base of each leaf Fruits that include capsules, follicles, or achenes A taproot system The Rosaceae family includes 4,828 known species of flowering plants in 91 genera.

Rose

A rose is a plant in the genus Rosa. There are over 300 species of roses and tens of thousands of cultivars. Roses are perennial shrubs that are often grown as ornamental plants for their beautiful and sometimes fragrant flowers

Apple

The scientific name for the apple is Malus pumila. It's also known as: Malus domestica, Malus sylvestris, Malus communis, Pyrus malus.The apple is a genus of about 30–35 species of small deciduous trees or shrubs in the flowering plant family Rosaceae.
