Moraceae

The scientific name of the banyan tree is Ficus benghalensis. It belongs to the fig family, scientifically known as Moraceae. The banyan tree is also known as the Indian banyan or banyan fig. The banyan tree is native to the Indian subcontinent. It can reach a height of up to 30 meters (100 feet) and spreads laterally indefinitely. It's a type of plant that grows on another plant by germinating seeds in a crack of a host tree. This process is called epiphyte. The banyan tree's bark and leaves both possess analgesic and anti-inflammatory properties. The bark of the Banyan tree is considered useful in: Burning sensation, Ulcers, Painful skin diseases, Inflammation, Toothache.

Banyan 

The banyan tree (Ficus benghalensis) is India's national tree. It's also known as the Indian banyan or banyan fig. The banyan tree is native to the Indian subcontinent and can grow up to 100 feet (30 m) tall. It spreads indefinitely and can live and regenerate for thousands of years.
